Task Three: Usability Test Report

Purpose of the study

        The goal of the website is to provide information for New Zealand Based gardeners and to help them manage their patio gardens.
The entire design and usability was focused around the key goal of providing organized information rather than a commercial purpose.
Given this presumption, my design reflects the end goal of the website. When the user arrives on the homepage, they are greeted with
a well organized navigation menu and automatically sliding images. These visual cues help people understand that Patio Green is an
information based website rather than a blog for marketing purposes. As a simple information based website, I will choose to analyze
two components of Jakob Neilson's heuristic analysis in relation to my website. Since the website does not use any advanced features
which means that many of the question about error handling, recovery and user control are not needed in this analysis. I used Morae
as means to record my tester's browsing experience as an account in order to document any findings and issues. Since in this case, my
primary stakeholder is my tester. I will look at "Consistency and Standards" and "Flexibility and Efficiency of Use".

Findings and Corrections

  • Consistency and Standards - Ease of Learning

    •         After a user arrives on the homepage, the navigation structure is intuitive and non-obstructive. It replicates systems other websites
    use - a title at the top of the page, a drop-down navigation system. This is to decrease any friction that user may face using the site,
    meaning that they can rely on their memory of other websites to guide them around rather than scrutinize the website to figure out what
    to do. Once they enter into the website, the basic interface they encounter remains the same - the same header, navigation, and sidebars,
    are often used to distinguish between different areas of a website. Patio green is not that advanced and does not require any changes in
    visual continuity to make this point to the user.

  • Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

    •         During the usability testing phase, I have discovered a few usability issues with regards to my Guide page, in the testing video, from
    the testing video, I noticed that after after viewing the article, users ought to be given the right to comment on each article, so that
    it allows each one of user to be able to share their thoughts, so a text field that allows them to contribute ideas would be necessary to have.
    Taken as a whole, the simple, familiar layout of our website allows the content of four website to shine rather than confuse users with too much
    information.

            Last but not least, one of the features which has an impact to the efficiency of use is the adoption of responsive design.
    The responsive design maintains the aspect ratio of the website when user perform enlargement and shrinkage of ther browser. More importantly,
    it provides an optimal viewing experience, much more easier for user to read and navigate with a munimum of resiszing, panning and scrolling.
    Although I did take the a risk with putting the light text on a light backgound, I mitigated many of the usability standards with color
    choice and layout design. In order to optimize the efficiency of use, I deem it is closely associated with the design, my goal was to avoid
    any disruptions in using the website so that users could immerse themselves in leanrning about the patio gardening information. I used a
    minimalist color scheme, replying only on emerald and aloe green to accentuate header text and links in the the shades of orange.
